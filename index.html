About Me

Ryû (Maker / Artist / Researcher) 2007年生まれ（19歳）。定量的・数理的アプローチの基盤を持ちながら、「数学、音楽、アートの境界の相対化」をコアテーマに、独立した研究・創作活動を展開する。現在経済学部2年次。

抽象的な数理概念や高次元の幾何学的構造を、視覚や聴覚といった異なる認知モダリティへと等価に翻訳することにより、知覚の相対化を促すアプローチを研究。音楽領域においては、スネアドラムのソロ楽器としての可能性を拡張すべく、演奏や作曲にとどまらず、モデリングに基づく楽器設計やブランド運営までを垂直統合的に手掛ける。

また、自身のバックグラウンドを生かし、日本におけるSTEAM教育およびギフテッド教育の新たなフレームワークを模索。学問・芸術・ものづくりの境界を越え、数理的思考と身体的感性の調和による新たな表現の創造と社会実装に取り組む。

About Me

Born in 2007 (19 years old). Operating on a rigorous quantitative and mathematical foundation, Ryû's core philosophy centers on "relativizing the boundaries between mathematics, music, and art" through independent research and creative practice. He is currently a second-year undergraduate in the Faculty of Economics (Department of Economic Management) at Kyushu University.

His research focuses on a cross-modal translation layer: mapping abstract mathematical concepts and high-dimensional geometric structures into equivalent visual and auditory modalities to challenge and expand human perception. In the realm of music, he redefines the snare drum as a standalone solo instrument. His approach is strictly vertically integrated—encompassing award-winning performance and composition, mathematical acoustic modeling, physical instrument architecture, and brand management.

Leveraging his unique background, he is also exploring new paradigms for STEAM and gifted education in Japan. By dissolving the borders between academia, art, and craft, he aims to realize new modes of expression and social implementation that harmonize mathematical reasoning with somatic sensibility.

Core Research Themes

Sonification of Analytic Number Theory

Investigating the structural correspondence between the distribution of complex functions and acoustic parameters to unlock new perceptual entry points into pure mathematics.

Cross-Modal Mapping of High-Dimensional Geometries

Projecting high-dimensional structures, such as the E_8 root system, onto a 2D plane through dynamic rotation and geodesic interpolation on Grassmann manifolds. By co-generating visual and auditory fields, this research liberates abstract symmetry from pure symbolic notation, allowing researchers to perceive it intuitively through unified sensorimotor feedback.

Algorithmic Composition & Mathematical Frameworks

Developing compositional algorithms rooted in Iannis Xenakis's stochastic music theory, Markov chains, group theory, and non-Euclidean geometries to generate complex temporal art.

Acoustic Physics & Generative Instrument Design

Evaluating the physical profiles of percussion instruments (vibrational modes, decay rates, frequency spectrums) through empirical experimentation and physical modeling. This data-driven loop informs RYU Percussion design choices, turning acoustic properties into a deliberate variable of creative intent.
